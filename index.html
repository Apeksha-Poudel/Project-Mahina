Who We Are

A movement born from empathy and courage

Project Mahina is a youth-led initiative aimed at breaking the silence and stigma around menstruation in rural Nepal. Founded by young people who believe change starts from within communities, we work to ensure every girl has the knowledge, resources, and safe space she deserves.


We believe that every girl deserves accurate knowledge, proper resources, and a safe space to grow with confidence and dignity — regardless of where she was born.

"Together, let's make every month a step towards empowerment."

Our Mission

What Project Mahina is doing

📚

Educate

We conduct interactive sessions to educate young girls about menstruation, the body, and menstrual hygiene — in a safe, open, and supportive environment.

🎀

Provide Resources

We collect funds to distribute cloth pads, sanitary pads, and other essential items to girls who lack access to basic menstrual care products.

💬

Break the Stigma

We create safe spaces for open conversations and actively work towards changing the deep-rooted cultural taboos surrounding menstruation.

🤝

Empower Communities

We involve local volunteers and schools to create a sustainable, community-driven impact that lasts beyond our visits.
